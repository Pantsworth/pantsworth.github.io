POTSTICKERS

Based on a recipe from: Serious Eats

Michael's Notes:
This is not an exact version of the serious eats recipe, but more of a basic version that avoids dealing with cabbage. Still tasty and pretty easy to make.

INGREDIENTS


1 lb Pork
7 Scallions, white and green parts separated, both minced
2 tablespoons grated/minced ginger (fresh)
2 cloves garlic, pressed or minced
2 tbsp soy sauce
Salt
Pepper
1/2 tsp Cayenne/Chili powder
40ish wonton wrappers
1 large egg, beaten
YIELD: 30-40 potstickers
TIME: 1 hour

DIRECTIONS


1. Combine the pork, white scallions and 1/4 of the green scallions, ginger, soy sauce, salt, pepper, cayenne, and garlic in a bowl.

2. Working 4-6 at a time, take out the wonton wrappers, place on cutting board. Coat half the circumference with egg. Add slightly less than 1 tbsp of the meat mixture to each.

3. To cook, add 2 tablespoons of oil to a large skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the potstickers in 3 batches, browning each batch on both sides, then removing and placing on a paper towel lined plate. When all are browned, set the heat on the skillet to medium-low. Add all the potstickers to the skillet and add 1 cup of water. Cover the skillet and let steam while occasionally sliding back and forth until all potstickers are cooked through, 4-5 minutes.
